Henry Mancini (1924–1994) was an American composer, conductor, and arranger raised in Aliquippa, just outside [[location:pittsburgh]]. He studied at the Aliquippa High School band program before Juilliard and a wartime stint in the Glenn Miller-Tex Beneke band, then built a defining career in Hollywood — *The Pink Panther*, *Peter Gunn*, *Breakfast at Tiffany's* ("Moon River"), *Days of Wine and Roses* — that fused jazz language with film scoring. He won four Academy Awards and twenty Grammy Awards.
