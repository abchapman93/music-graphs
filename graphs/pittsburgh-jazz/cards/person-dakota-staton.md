Dakota Staton was a jazz vocalist born in [[location:pittsburgh]] in 1930 whose 1957 Capitol album *The Late, Late Show* — driven by her bluesy phrasing, swing-era timing, and the arrangements of Van Alexander and Sid Feller — became a standard-bearer of late-1950s vocal jazz, reaching No. 4 on the Billboard album chart.
