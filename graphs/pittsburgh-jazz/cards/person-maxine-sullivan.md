Maxine Sullivan was a swing-era vocalist born in Homestead — a steel town adjacent to [[location:pittsburgh]] — in 1911, whose understated, gently swinging delivery made her one of the most distinctive jazz singers of the late 1930s. Her 1937 hit recording of "Loch Lomond" with Claude Thornhill's arrangement launched a long career interpreting the Great American Songbook and adapted folk tunes.
