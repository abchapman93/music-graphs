"Blood Count" is the [[person:billy-strayhorn]] composition that became one of jazz's most affecting elegies — Strayhorn wrote it from his hospital bed in the weeks before his death in 1967, and [[person:duke-ellington]]'s orchestra recorded the definitive version on the tribute album *...And His Mother Called Him Bill*.
