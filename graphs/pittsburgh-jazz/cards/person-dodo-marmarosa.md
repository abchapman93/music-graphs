Dodo Marmarosa was a pianist born in [[location:pittsburgh]] in 1925 who, despite a brief career, played a key role in early bebop. As a teenager he toured with Gene Krupa and Tommy Dorsey, then moved to Los Angeles in the mid-1940s where he recorded with Charlie Parker on the legendary Dial sessions and accompanied Lester Young. Mental-health struggles cut short his New York years; he returned to Pittsburgh in semi-retirement.
