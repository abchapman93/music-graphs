"Weather Bird" is the December 1928 duet by [[person:louis-armstrong]] (cornet) and [[person:earl-hines]] (piano) — widely regarded as the first masterpiece of duo improvisation in recorded jazz and the moment two soloists demonstrated that jazz dialogue could rise to the level of composition.
