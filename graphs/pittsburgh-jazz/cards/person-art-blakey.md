Art Blakey was an American jazz drummer and bandleader born in [[location:pittsburgh]], one of the architects of hard bop and among the most influential drummers in jazz history. He developed a powerful, swinging style anchored by a thunderous press roll and the propulsive use of the hi-hat on beats two and four. After early work with Mary Lou Williams, Fletcher Henderson, and Billy Eckstine's bebop big band — where he met Charlie Parker, Dizzy Gillespie, and Miles Davis — Blakey co-founded The Jazz Messengers in the mid-1950s with pianist Horace Silver. The Messengers became the most important finishing school in modern jazz; over four decades Blakey hired and developed generations of musicians including Wayne Shorter, Lee Morgan, Freddie Hubbard, Bobby Timmons, Keith Jarrett, Wynton Marsalis, and Branford Marsalis. The group's 1958 album [[album:moanin]] remains the canonical hard bop statement. Blakey kept The Jazz Messengers active until shortly before his death in 1990.
