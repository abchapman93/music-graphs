Erroll Garner was an American jazz pianist and composer born in [[location:pittsburgh]]'s East Liberty neighborhood. Self-taught and famously unable to read music, he developed an instantly recognizable style: rolling left-hand chords behind a buoyant right-hand line that often anticipated or trailed the beat, giving his playing a uniquely loose, swinging feel. He came up in the Hill District jazz scene that also nurtured [[person:earl-hines]] and [[person:billy-strayhorn]] before moving to New York in the 1940s. His 1955 live album *Concert by the Sea* became one of the best-selling jazz records of its era. Garner composed the standard "Misty," which became a hit for Johnny Mathis and was later covered by hundreds of artists. He toured prolifically and was the first jazz artist to be managed by impresario Sol Hurok, which brought him to concert-hall audiences worldwide. His warmth, accessibility, and rhythmic invention made him one of the most beloved pianists in jazz.
