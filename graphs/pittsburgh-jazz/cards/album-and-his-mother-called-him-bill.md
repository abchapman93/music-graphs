*...And His Mother Called Him Bill* (1968, RCA Victor) is Duke Ellington's tribute to [[person:billy-strayhorn|Billy Strayhorn]], recorded months after Strayhorn's death from cancer in 1967. The Ellington Orchestra performs a program almost entirely of Strayhorn compositions — "Blood Count" (written from his hospital bed), "U.M.M.G.", "Lotus Blossom", "Day Dream". The album closes with Ellington alone at the piano playing "Lotus Blossom", caught informally as the session was breaking down.
