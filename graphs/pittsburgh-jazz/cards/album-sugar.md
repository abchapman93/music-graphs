*Sugar* is a 1970 album by [[person:stanley-turrentine]], his first for Creed Taylor's CTI Records and a landmark of the soul-jazz / crossover sound that defined the label's early-1970s catalog. Recorded with a stellar cast that included Freddie Hubbard on trumpet and George Benson on guitar, the album's relaxed grooves and Turrentine's vocalized tenor sound made it one of the best-selling jazz albums of its year. The title track, [[track:sugar-track]], became an instant standard and has been covered by dozens of artists since. The album is widely credited with helping to bridge jazz and rhythm and blues audiences in the early 1970s.
