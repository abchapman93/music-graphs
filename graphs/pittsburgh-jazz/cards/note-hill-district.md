The Hill District is a historically Black neighborhood east of downtown [[location:pittsburgh]] that, from roughly the 1920s through the 1950s, was one of the most fertile jazz communities in the United States. A combination of factors — the Great Migration, segregation that concentrated Black social life in the Hill, vibrant nightclubs (Crawford Grill, Hurricane Lounge, Savoy Ballroom), and a deep church and lodge music tradition — produced an astonishing concentration of major jazz musicians in a small area. [[person:earl-hines]], [[person:billy-strayhorn]], [[person:art-blakey]], [[person:erroll-garner]], [[person:stanley-turrentine]], and [[person:ray-brown]] all came up in or near the Hill. Urban renewal projects in the 1950s and 1960s, particularly the construction of the Civic Arena, displaced thousands of residents and effectively destroyed the lower Hill's club scene. The neighborhood's musical legacy is preserved through the August Wilson Cycle of plays, the Pittsburgh Jazz Society, and the continuing work of musicians like [[person:roger-humphries]] and [[person:sean-jones]].
