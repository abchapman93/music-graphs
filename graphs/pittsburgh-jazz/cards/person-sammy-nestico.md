Sammy Nestico (1924–2021) was an American composer, arranger, and trombonist born in [[location:pittsburgh]]. He is best known as the principal arranger for the Count Basie Orchestra from 1968 through the 1980s, writing classic charts like "Basie — Straight Ahead" and "Warm Breeze" that helped define the late-period Basie sound. Nestico also arranged for the U.S. Air Force "Airmen of Note" and for studio sessions with Sinatra, Sarah Vaughan, and Bing Crosby, and his big-band method books became standard texts in jazz education.
