Paul Chambers was an American jazz double bassist, best known as a member of the classic Miles Davis Quintet and Sextet from 1955 to 1963. Though born in [[location:pittsburgh]], he grew up in Detroit, where he came up alongside a remarkable generation of musicians. He arrived in New York in 1954 and within a year was playing with Davis, contributing to landmark albums including *Round About Midnight*, *Milestones*, and *Kind of Blue*. Chambers' walking lines were rhythmically perfect and harmonically rich, and he was one of the first bassists to take improvised solos using a bow (arco) on bebop changes. He played on a huge fraction of the great Blue Note and Prestige sessions of the late 1950s, including John Coltrane's *Blue Train* — Coltrane's "Mr. P.C." is named for him. Chambers worked frequently with [[person:art-blakey]] in pickup groups, and he tied the Pittsburgh hard bop world to the Detroit and New York scenes. He died young, of tuberculosis, in 1969.
