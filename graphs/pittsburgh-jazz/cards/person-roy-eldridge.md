Roy Eldridge was a trumpeter born in [[location:pittsburgh]] in 1911 whose hot, high-register attack bridged Louis Armstrong's swing-era trumpet and Dizzy Gillespie's bebop. Known as "Little Jazz," he toured with Gene Krupa and Artie Shaw — among the first Black soloists to integrate major white big bands — and remained a fierce, competitive blower well into the 1970s.
