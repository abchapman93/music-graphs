Roger Humphries is an American jazz drummer born and raised in [[location:pittsburgh]]. A child prodigy who was already gigging professionally as a young teenager, he came up in the same Hill District jazz community that produced [[person:art-blakey]], [[person:stanley-turrentine]], and [[person:ray-brown]]. Humphries gained national attention in the mid-1960s as the drummer on several important Horace Silver Quintet albums, including the much-loved 1965 release *Song for My Father*, where his crisp, dancing time helped define the record's signature feel. After leaving Silver, Humphries returned to Pittsburgh, where he chose to base his career rather than relocate permanently to New York. He has spent decades as the city's pre-eminent jazz drummer, leading his own groups, mentoring younger players including [[person:sean-jones]], and teaching. He remains one of the strongest living links between the Hill District tradition and contemporary Pittsburgh jazz.
