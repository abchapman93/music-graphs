Sean Jones is an American jazz trumpeter, composer, and educator, born in Warren, Ohio and long associated with [[location:pittsburgh]] — first as a student at Duquesne University, then as a returning faculty member and central figure in the city's contemporary jazz scene. He came to prominence in the early 2000s when Wynton Marsalis hired him as lead trumpet of the Jazz at Lincoln Center Orchestra, a position he held for six years. As a leader he has released a series of well-received albums on the Mack Avenue label, demonstrating a deep grounding in the hard bop tradition of [[person:art-blakey]] alongside a contemporary, harmonically adventurous voice of his own. Jones is also one of the most visible jazz educators of his generation: he chaired the Brass Department at Berklee College of Music, and he later joined the Peabody Conservatory of Johns Hopkins University as chair of jazz studies. His mentorship by Pittsburgh elders including [[person:roger-humphries]] places him directly in the city's living jazz lineage.
