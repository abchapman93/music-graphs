*Song for My Father* (1965, Blue Note) is the Horace Silver Quintet's signature album, with [[person:roger-humphries|Roger Humphries]] on drums alongside Carmell Jones (trumpet) and Joe Henderson (tenor sax). The title track — built on a bossa-nova rhythm and a memorable bass figure later borrowed by Steely Dan's "Rikki Don't Lose That Number" — became one of the most enduring hard-bop compositions. Humphries was 21 when the session was recorded.
