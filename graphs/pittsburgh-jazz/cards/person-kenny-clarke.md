Kenny Clarke was a drummer born in [[location:pittsburgh]] in 1914 who, more than any other musician, invented modern jazz drumming. At Minton's Playhouse in the early 1940s he shifted timekeeping from the bass drum to the ride cymbal — freeing the bass and snare for unpredictable accents — and was a co-founder of the Modern Jazz Quartet with John Lewis, Milt Jackson, and Percy Heath.
