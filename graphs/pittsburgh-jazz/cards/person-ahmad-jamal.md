Ahmad Jamal was a pianist born in [[location:pittsburgh]] in 1930 whose spacious, lyrical trio style transformed mid-20th-century jazz piano. His 1958 hit recording of "Poinciana" — built on suspended grooves, dynamic restraint, and dramatic use of silence — became a model for Miles Davis's First Great Quintet and influenced generations of pianists.
