Mary Lou Williams was a pianist, composer, and arranger raised in [[location:pittsburgh]]'s East Liberty neighborhood, and one of the few musicians whose work spanned and shaped multiple jazz eras. She wrote and arranged for Andy Kirk's Twelve Clouds of Joy in the 1930s, Benny Goodman and Duke Ellington in the 1940s, and mentored Thelonious Monk, Bud Powell, and Dizzy Gillespie at her Harlem apartment during the birth of bebop.
