Gene Harris was a soul-jazz pianist born in Benton Harbor, Michigan in 1933, best known for his long collaboration with bassist [[person:ray-brown]] and a discography rooted in blues, gospel, and bebop. The [[album:gene-harris-trio-plus-one]] recording on Concord is a representative document of his trio work and a key node connecting [[location:pittsburgh]]'s Ray Brown to the broader West Coast soul-jazz scene.
