Duke Ellington was the bandleader, pianist, and composer who, more than any other figure, established jazz as a serious American art form. His Pittsburgh tie is through [[person:billy-strayhorn]], who joined Ellington's organization in 1939 and remained his closest musical collaborator until Strayhorn's death in 1967 — co-composing or arranging much of the late-period Ellington book.
