Billy Eckstine was a baritone vocalist and bandleader born in [[location:pittsburgh]] in 1914 whose 1944–47 big band was the first to fully embrace bebop. The band's roster — Charlie Parker, Dizzy Gillespie, Dexter Gordon, Miles Davis, Sarah Vaughan, and [[person:art-blakey]] on drums — reads like a founding charter of modern jazz, and Eckstine's velvet baritone made him one of the era's most popular Black vocalists.
