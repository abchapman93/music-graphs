"Poinciana" is Ahmad Jamal's signature recording, first released on the 1958 live album *At the Pershing: But Not for Me*. Its hypnotic, suspended groove — bass and brushes locked into a slow vamp while Jamal's right hand explores the melody with patient understatement — became one of the most influential piano-trio statements of the era.
