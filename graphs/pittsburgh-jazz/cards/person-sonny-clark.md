Sonny Clark was a bebop pianist born in Herminie, Pennsylvania (a coal-mining town outside [[location:pittsburgh]]) in 1931, who formed his early style in Pittsburgh clubs before moving to Los Angeles and then New York. Between 1957 and his early death in 1963 he led a series of canonical Blue Note sessions — *Cool Struttin'*, *Sonny's Crib*, *Leapin' and Lopin'* — that distilled hard bop piano into bluesy, taut, and impeccably swinging statements.
