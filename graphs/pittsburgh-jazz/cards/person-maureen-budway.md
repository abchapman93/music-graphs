Maureen Budway was an American jazz vocalist based in [[location:pittsburgh]], widely regarded as one of the finest singers in the city's modern jazz scene. She came from a deeply musical Pittsburgh family — her brother, pianist David Budway, played frequently in her groups and went on to a distinguished career of his own. Maureen worked her way up through the Pittsburgh club circuit and became a fixture at venues including the Backstage Bar, where her warm, swinging delivery and sophisticated repertoire drew local musicians and audiences for decades. She collaborated with virtually every important Pittsburgh jazz musician of her era, including [[person:roger-humphries]]. Although she rarely toured outside the region, her recordings — including several with her brother — preserved a vocal style rooted in the great songbook tradition and the bebop-derived phrasing of singers like Carmen McRae and Sarah Vaughan. She died of breast cancer in 2013, leaving a legacy that the local jazz community continues to honor.

Maureen Budway recorded [[album:sweet-candor|Sweet Candor]].

Alec accompanied her voice lessons when she taught at Duquesne University. *Sweet Candor* was recorded shortly before her death in 2013 and released posthumously on MCG Jazz in 2015 — the album's reading of Stephen Foster's "Hard Times Come Again No More" carries an additional weight in that context.
