Ray Brown was an American jazz double bassist, born in [[location:pittsburgh]] and widely regarded as one of the greatest bassists in jazz history. He arrived in New York in 1945 and was almost immediately recruited by Dizzy Gillespie for his groundbreaking bebop big band, where he played alongside Charlie Parker and a young [[person:art-blakey]]. Brown was a foundational member of the Modern Jazz Quartet in its earliest incarnation and was married to Ella Fitzgerald from 1947 to 1953. From 1951 to 1966 he was the bassist in the Oscar Peterson Trio, an association that produced dozens of definitive albums and set the standard for piano-trio bass playing. His tone was huge, his time impeccable, and his note choices behind soloists endlessly inventive. After leaving Peterson he settled in Los Angeles, ran a successful career as a studio musician and producer, and continued leading his own trios into the 21st century. His Pittsburgh upbringing places him squarely in the Hill District lineage that also produced [[person:paul-chambers]] (by birth) and [[person:billy-strayhorn]].
