"After You've Gone" is a 1918 popular song that became one of [[person:roy-eldridge]]'s signature showpieces; his high-register, breakneck improvisations on the tune helped define the swing-era trumpet vocabulary that Dizzy Gillespie would soon extend into bebop. Related: [[location:pittsburgh]].
