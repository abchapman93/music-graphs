Louis Armstrong (1901–1971) was the New Orleans–born trumpeter and vocalist who, more than any other musician, established the soloist as the center of jazz. His Pittsburgh connection runs through his late-1920s partnership with [[person:earl-hines]] in Chicago — the Hot Five and Hot Seven sessions, plus the canonical 1928 Armstrong–Hines duet "Weather Bird," widely considered the first masterpiece of duo improvisation in recorded jazz.
