Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania — a major incubator of mid-century jazz. The Hill District, in particular, produced an outsized number of nationally significant musicians from the 1930s through the 1960s. Local clubs like the Crawford Grill and the Hurricane Lounge gave young players steady work and a tight community. The city's jazz lineage runs through [[person:earl-hines]] and [[person:billy-strayhorn]] in the swing era, through bop-era figures like [[person:art-blakey]], [[person:errol-garner]], [[person:ray-brown]], and [[person:paul-chambers]], and into the soul-jazz and hard bop era with [[person:stanley-turrentine]] and [[person:roger-humphries]]. Later generations including [[person:maureen-budway]] and [[person:sean-jones]] continued the tradition.
