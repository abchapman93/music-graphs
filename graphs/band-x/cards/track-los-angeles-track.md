Title track of the [[album:los-angeles]] album by [[group:x]], one of the band's signature songs and a vivid, harsh portrait of late-1970s Los Angeles, sung in close harmony by [[person:exene-cervenka]] and [[person:john-doe]]. Built on a driving rockabilly riff from [[person:billy-zoom]] and the propulsive backbeat of [[person:dj-bonebrake]], the song's lyrics — about a young woman fleeing the city she has come to hate — capture the literary, image-driven approach that set X's songwriting apart from much of the punk scene of the era.
