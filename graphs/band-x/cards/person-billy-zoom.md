Billy Zoom (born Tyson Kindell) is an American guitarist, best known as the founding guitarist of the Los Angeles punk band [[group:x]]. Older than the rest of the band and with a deep grounding in 1950s rockabilly and rhythm and blues, Zoom had played with Gene Vincent and other rock-and-roll veterans before answering an ad placed by [[person:john-doe]] in 1977. His clean, propulsive, rockabilly-derived lead playing on a 1950s Gretsch — and his signature stage presence, standing rooted in place while grinning widely at the audience — gave X a musical and visual identity distinct from the noisier wings of L.A. punk. He left X in 1985, returned in 1998, and has remained with the band through its 21st-century reunions. Outside X he has built and repaired instruments and amplifiers professionally for decades; his shop is well known among guitarists and other musicians. He has been treated for cancer in recent years and continues to tour.
