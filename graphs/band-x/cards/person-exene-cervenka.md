Exene Cervenka (born Christene Lee Cervenka) is an American singer, songwriter, poet, and visual artist, best known as the co-lead vocalist and lyricist of the Los Angeles punk band [[group:x]]. She moved to Los Angeles in the mid-1970s and met [[person:john-doe]] at a Venice poetry workshop; their collaboration on lyrics — drawing on Beat poetry, gothic Americana, and the textures of L.A. street life — became the literary core of X's sound. Their famously close, dissonant vocal harmonies are one of the most recognizable signatures in American punk. Cervenka and Doe were married from 1980 to 1985 and continued to work together as bandmates and friends after their divorce. Outside X she has worked extensively as a solo artist, recorded country-folk material with Doe and others, and co-founded the all-female roots band The Knitters with Doe, [[person:dj-bonebrake]], and [[person:dave-alvin]]. She is also a prolific visual artist and collage maker, with work that draws on the same Americana imagery as her lyrics.

Exene Cervenka recorded [[album:somewhere-gone|Somewhere Gone]].
