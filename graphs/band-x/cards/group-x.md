X is an American punk rock band formed in Los Angeles in 1977. Its longest-running lineup is [[person:exene-cervenka]] on vocals, [[person:john-doe]] on bass and vocals, [[person:billy-zoom]] on guitar, and [[person:dj-bonebrake]] on drums. X was a defining band of the L.A. punk scene of the late 1970s and early 1980s, but unlike many of their hardcore-leaning peers, they drew heavily on country, rockabilly, blues, and the Beat poetry tradition. The interlocking vocal harmonies of Cervenka and Doe — close, often dissonant, sometimes plainly off-key in ways that became their signature — sit on top of Zoom's clean, propulsive rockabilly-derived guitar and Bonebrake's swinging drumming. Their first four albums, all produced by The Doors' Ray Manzarek, are considered classics: [[album:los-angeles]] (1980), [[album:wild-gift]] (1981), [[album:under-the-big-black-sun]] (1982), and *More Fun in the New World* (1983). [[person:dave-alvin]] briefly replaced Zoom on guitar in 1985–86, and [[person:tony-gilkyson]] held the lead-guitar chair from 1986 to 1995. After a hiatus through much of the 1990s, the original lineup reunited and has continued to tour and record into the 2020s, including the album *Alphabetland* (2020).
