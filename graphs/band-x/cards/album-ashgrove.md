*Ashgrove* (2004) is Dave Alvin's seventh solo album, named after the legendary Los Angeles folk club where Alvin first saw the blues, country, and folk artists who shaped his songwriting. The record is a literate, road-tested set of originals about California history, family loss, and working-class memory; the title track is a meditation on the now-shuttered club and the bluesmen Alvin saw there as a kid. *Ashgrove* was nominated for the Grammy for Best Contemporary Folk Album in 2005.
