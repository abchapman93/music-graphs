Dave Alvin is an American singer-songwriter and guitarist, best known as the lead guitarist of the roots-rock band The Blasters, which he co-founded with his older brother Phil Alvin in Downey, California in 1979. The Blasters shared bills and a scene with [[group:x]] and other L.A. punk bands in the early 1980s, blending rockabilly, blues, R&B, and country into a hard, electric sound. Alvin briefly joined X as guitarist in 1985–86, replacing [[person:billy-zoom]], and contributed to the album *See How We Are*. Around the same time he became a founding member of The Knitters, the acoustic country-folk side project led by [[person:john-doe]] and [[person:exene-cervenka]] with [[person:dj-bonebrake]] on drums. Since leaving X to pursue a solo career, Alvin has released a long series of acclaimed Americana albums; his 2000 record *Public Domain: Songs from the Wild Land* won the Grammy for Best Contemporary Folk Album. He continues to tour and record in 2020s.
