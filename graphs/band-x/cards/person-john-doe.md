John Doe (born John Nommensen Duchac) is an American singer, songwriter, bassist, and actor, best known as a founding member of the Los Angeles punk band [[group:x]]. Originally from Decatur, Illinois, he moved to Los Angeles in the mid-1970s and met [[person:exene-cervenka]] at a Venice poetry workshop; the two became creative partners, then bandmates, then briefly married. As X's bassist and co-lead vocalist, Doe contributed the deeper, steadier counter-voice to Cervenka's higher, edgier line — together they produced the close, slightly-off harmonies that define the band. Outside X he has maintained a long parallel career as a solo artist working in a country, folk, and Americana vein, with a series of well-received albums beginning with *Meet John Doe* in 1990. He is a founding member of The Knitters, the country-folk side project that also features Cervenka, [[person:dj-bonebrake]], and [[person:dave-alvin]]. Doe has also worked extensively as a character actor in film and television since the late 1980s.

John Doe recorded [[album:a-year-in-the-wilderness|A Year in the Wilderness]].
