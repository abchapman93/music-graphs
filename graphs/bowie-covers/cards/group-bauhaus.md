Bauhaus was a British post-punk band formed in Northampton in 1978, widely considered one of the founding groups of the gothic rock genre. Their [[album:ziggy-stardust-bauhaus-1982]] — a cover of [[person:david-bowie]]'s [[song:ziggy-stardust]] — became one of the most celebrated cover recordings in post-punk history. Bauhaus transformed the glam-rock anthem into a slow, cavernous gothic dirge, with Peter Murphy's dramatic baritone and the band's minimal, shadowy arrangement giving the song an entirely new emotional character.
