David Bowie (born David Robert Jones) was an English singer, songwriter, and actor, one of the most influential musicians of the late 20th century. Over a five-decade career, he moved through a remarkable series of personae and styles — folk troubadour, glam-rock alien Ziggy Stardust, soul singer, Berlin-era avant-pop experimentalist, mainstream pop superstar of the 1980s, and elder statesman of art rock — and shaped, in each, the sound of the music around him. His best-known songs include [[song:life-on-mars]], "Space Oddity," "Heroes," "Changes," "Starman," "Ashes to Ashes," "Let's Dance," and "Modern Love." His final album, [[album:blackstar]] (2016), was released two days before his death from cancer and is widely regarded as one of the most extraordinary farewell statements in popular music. Bowie's songs have been covered across an enormous stylistic range: by [[group:nirvana]] in an MTV Unplugged session, by Brazilian guitarist [[person:seu-jorge]] in Portuguese for a Wes Anderson film, and by the jazz piano trio [[group:bad-plus]], among many others. His openness to other artists' interpretations — and his own career-long appetite for borrowing across genres — helped make his catalog one of the most-covered in modern music.
