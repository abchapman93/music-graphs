"Life on Mars?" is a song written by [[person:david-bowie]] and released on his 1971 album [[album:hunky-dory]]. Built around a soaring piano part by Rick Wakeman and a string arrangement by Mick Ronson, the song's surreal narrative — a "girl with the mousy hair" watching a banal Hollywood movie that turns into a cosmic vision — set against one of Bowie's most dramatic vocal performances has made it one of his most beloved compositions. Bowie wrote the music as a deliberate response to having submitted English lyrics for the French song "Comme d'habitude," only to see Paul Anka acquire the rights and turn it into "My Way" for Frank Sinatra. "Life on Mars?" has been covered by many artists across genres, including a jazz-trio reading by [[group:bad-plus]] on their album [[album:these-are-the-vistas]]. The song's title was also adopted for a 2006 BBC television drama, which featured Bowie's original recording in its opening sequence.
