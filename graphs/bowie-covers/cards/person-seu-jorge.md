Seu Jorge (born Jorge Mário da Silva) is a Brazilian singer, songwriter, guitarist, and actor from Rio de Janeiro. He came to international attention through his role as the Brazilian sailor Pelé dos Santos in Wes Anderson's 2004 film *The Life Aquatic with Steve Zissou*, in which his character spent much of the film sitting on the deck of the research vessel and singing acoustic, Portuguese-language versions of [[person:david-bowie]] songs. Those covers, recorded for the film and released on the soundtrack album [[album:life-aquatic-studio-sessions]], became a phenomenon in their own right — Bowie himself praised them, reportedly saying that hearing his songs sung that way gave them "a new level of beauty." Jorge's reinterpretations of [[song:rebel-rebel]], "Starman," "Life on Mars?," "Changes," and "Suffragette City" stripped them down to nylon-string guitar and a soft Brazilian vocal idiom. He has continued a parallel career as one of Brazil's most popular contemporary singers and as an actor in films including *City of God*.
