"The Man Who Sold the World" is a song written by [[person:david-bowie]] and the title track of his 1970 album of the same name. Built on a circular descending guitar riff by Mick Ronson, the song's enigmatic lyrics about a meeting with a doppelgänger have invited decades of interpretation. It became internationally famous in 1993 when [[group:nirvana]] performed it on their *MTV Unplugged in New York* session — the cover was released the following year on the [[album:unplugged-in-new-york]] live album, after Kurt Cobain's death, and introduced the song to an enormous new audience. Bowie himself later said that he was often asked to perform "his cover" of Nirvana's song, an irony he found amusing.
