Philip Glass (born 1937) is an American composer and one of the most influential figures in minimalist music. His connection to [[person:david-bowie]] runs deep: both orbited Brian Eno's Berlin creative circle in the late 1970s, and Bowie's *Heroes* and *Low* albums were direct influences on Glass's own developing aesthetic. Glass formalized this relationship by adapting the entire *Heroes* album as [[album:symphony-no-4-heroes]] in 1996 — a full symphonic treatment of Bowie and Eno's electronic compositions that remains one of the most substantial classical responses to a pop album ever written.
