Mott the Hoople was a British rock band active from 1969 to 1974, led by vocalist Ian Hunter. Though critically respected, the band was on the verge of breaking up in 1972 when [[person:david-bowie]] intervened, offering them [[song:all-the-young-dudes]], producing their recording, and relaunching their career. The resulting album [[album:all-the-young-dudes-album]] became their commercial breakthrough and one of the definitive documents of British glam rock. Bowie's gift — writing and producing a song that perfectly suited Hunter's voice and the band's sensibility — is one of the more striking acts of musical generosity in rock history.
