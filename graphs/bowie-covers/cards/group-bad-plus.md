The Bad Plus is an American jazz trio formed in 2000. Its original lineup — pianist Ethan Iverson, bassist Reid Anderson, and drummer Dave King — became one of the most prominent jazz groups of the 2000s, in part through inventive, fully-committed instrumental covers of rock and pop songs alongside original material. Their second album, [[album:these-are-the-vistas]] (2003), included readings of [[person:david-bowie]]'s [[song:life-on-mars]] alongside Nirvana's "Smells Like Teen Spirit" and Blondie's "Heart of Glass," and helped establish them with audiences far beyond the typical jazz-club circuit. They continued covering material across genres on subsequent albums. Iverson departed in 2017; the trio has continued in altered forms with the original two-thirds rhythm section still anchoring the group.
