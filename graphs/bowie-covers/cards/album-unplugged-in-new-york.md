*MTV Unplugged in New York* is a 1994 live album by [[group:nirvana]], drawn from the band's November 1993 acoustic performance for the MTV *Unplugged* series. Released after Kurt Cobain's death, the album was the band's first posthumous release and won the Grammy for Best Alternative Music Performance in 1996. It is widely regarded as one of the finest entries in the *Unplugged* series. The setlist deliberately avoided most of the band's hits in favor of deep cuts and covers, including [[song:the-man-who-sold-the-world]] by [[person:david-bowie]] and three songs by the Meat Puppets (with Cris and Curt Kirkwood joining the band).
