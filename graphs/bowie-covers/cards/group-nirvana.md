Nirvana was an American rock band formed in Aberdeen, Washington in 1987 by singer-guitarist Kurt Cobain and bassist Krist Novoselic, with drummer Dave Grohl joining in 1990. The band became one of the defining acts of the early 1990s through the breakthrough success of their 1991 album *Nevermind* and its single "Smells Like Teen Spirit." In 1993 the band performed an acoustic set for MTV's *Unplugged* series, released the following year as [[album:unplugged-in-new-york]], that closed with a haunting cover of [[person:david-bowie]]'s [[song:the-man-who-sold-the-world]]. The cover became one of the most widely heard Bowie versions outside of Bowie's own recordings; for many listeners of the grunge generation it was their first exposure to the song. The band ended with Cobain's death in 1994.
